## 1. Bulgarian SME Market Size & NIS2 Reality

### Hard Numbers

**Medium Enterprises (50-249 employees)**:
- **422,529 people employed** in medium-sized enterprises across Bulgaria ([Statista SME Employment](https://www.statista.com/statistics/936721/employment-by-smes-in-bulgaria/))
- Represents **20.5% of total employment**
- Total medium enterprises: Data requires Statista premium, estimated ~5,000-8,000 companies

**NIS2 Compliance Status**:
- **Bulgaria MISSED the October 17, 2024 deadline** - law still not transposed ([Copla NIS2 Bulgaria](https://copla.com/blog/compliance-regulations/nis2-directive-regulations-and-implementation-in-bulgaria/))
- Earliest realistic implementation: **Q3 2025** (if political stability returns)
- Expected full enforcement: **mid-2026** at earliest

**Reality Check**: NIS2 is NOT a burning pain point yet. SMEs are waiting to see what happens, not scrambling to comply.

### Who Must Comply?

**NIS2 Applies To**:
- Medium-sized and large entities in **critical sectors** (energy, transport, finance, healthcare, digital infrastructure)
- **Suppliers** to these industries

**The Problem**:
- Most Bulgarian SMEs are **NOT in critical sectors** - they're manufacturing, retail, services
- Only a **subset** of the 5,000-8,000 medium enterprises actually need NIS2
- **Realistic addressable market**: 500-1,000 companies maximum in Bulgaria

**Source**: [NIS2 Directive Bulgaria Implementation](https://copla.com/blog/compliance-regulations/nis2-directive-regulations-and-implementation-in-bulgaria/), [Protiviti Bulgaria NIS2](https://www.protiviti.com/bg-bg/nis2-compliance)

## 2. Current Compliance Reality: The Gap

### What Regulations Say SMEs Should Do

- GDPR: Data protection officer, consent management, breach notification within 72 hours
- NIS2: Risk assessments, incident response, supply chain protection, MFA, backups
- ISO 27001: Information security management system
- TISAX: Automotive sector cybersecurity

### What Bulgarian SMEs Actually Do

**From Research**:
- **Complaint-based enforcement** - Bulgarian CPDP doesn't proactively audit, they respond to complaints ([DLA Piper Bulgaria GDPR](https://www.dlapiperdataprotection.com/index.html?t=law&c=BG))
- **â‚¬1,000-10,000 spent on GDPR** in 2019 (one-time, not recurring) ([Statista GDPR Spending](https://www.statista.com/statistics/1176050/gdpr-compliance-spending-in-small-businesses-europe/))
- **CPDP provides self-assessment tool** and hotline for SMEs - free resources exist ([White & Case Bulgaria GDPR](https://www.whitecase.com/insight-our-thinking/gdpr-guide-national-implementation-bulgaria))

**What This Means**:
- SMEs do **minimal compliance** unless they get caught
- They use **free tools** and ask their accountant
- They pay **only when forced** (after complaint or audit)

### The "Excel and Hope" Reality

**Current SME Compliance Workflow**:
1. Owner asks accountant: "Are we compliant?"
2. Accountant says: "Probably, I'll ask my lawyer friend"
3. Lawyer friend says: "Make a privacy policy and hope"
4. SME downloads free template from CPDP website
5. Nothing happens until there's a complaint
6. **Then** they panic and call Protiviti or a law firm

**Your Challenge**: You're trying to sell **prevention** to companies who only buy **cure**.

## 4. The "Local Guy" Advantage - Your Only Real Edge

### Bulgarian Business Culture: Face-to-Face or Nothing

**Your Advantage**:
