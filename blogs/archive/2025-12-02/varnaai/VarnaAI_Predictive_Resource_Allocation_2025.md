**[Image 7 Placeholder]**
- **Alt Text:** Warning checklist showing five common implementation mistakes and solutions
- **Title:** Avoid These Five Implementation Pitfalls
- **Caption:** Learn from common mistakes: poor data quality, inadequate training, unrealistic expectations, insufficient change management, and neglecting compliance
- **Dimensions:** 850x550

### Mistake 1: Insufficient Historical Data

Organizations sometimes attempt deployment with limited historical data. Machine learning models require substantial training information. Forecast accuracy suffers significantly when historical data spans less than 12 months.

**Solution:** Delay implementation until adequate historical data exists. Alternatively, start with simplified models that require less training data. Expand to advanced forecasting as historical information accumulates.

### Mistake 2: Ignoring Change Management

Technical deployment succeeds but user adoption fails without proper change management. Project managers may resist automated recommendations. They continue manual processes despite available automation.

**Solution:** Invest heavily in change management and user training. Explain how technology augments rather than replaces human judgment. Demonstrate value through pilot projects before full deployment.

### Mistake 3: Unrealistic Accuracy Expectations

Some organizations expect perfect forecast accuracy immediately. Systems improve over time as models learn organizational patterns. Initial accuracy may be 70-80% rather than 95%+.

**Solution:** Set realistic accuracy expectations during deployment planning. Communicate that precision improves progressively over time. Celebrate incremental improvements rather than demanding perfection immediately.
