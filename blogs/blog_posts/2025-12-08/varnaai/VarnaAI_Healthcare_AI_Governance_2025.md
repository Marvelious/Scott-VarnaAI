<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Why Healthcare AI Requires Special Governance</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Healthcare AI governance differs fundamentally from general enterprise AI governance. Medical AI directly impacts human health and life, creating unique ethical obligations.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Regulatory requirements in healthcare are more stringent than most industries. HIPAA, FDA regulations, and medical device standards all apply to AI systems.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>According to <a href="https://www.who.int" target="_blank" rel="noopener">World Health Organization</a> guidelines, healthcare AI governance must prioritize patient welfare above efficiency gains or cost savings.</p>

<p>Healthcare AI governance mandates rigorous clinical validation before deployment. AI models must demonstrate safety and effectiveness in real medical environments.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Validation includes testing on diverse patient populations to ensure equitable performance. Models that work well for some demographics may fail for others.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Requirement 2: Explainability for Clinical Decisions</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Healthcare AI governance requires AI systems to explain their reasoning. Clinicians cannot accept AI recommendations without understanding the underlying logic.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Explainability enables clinicians to identify when AI outputs may be incorrect. Human judgment remains essential in healthcare AI governance frameworks.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Black-box models are generally unacceptable for clinical decisions. Healthcare AI governance favors interpretable models or requires explanation layers for complex systems.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Requirement 4: Continuous Performance Monitoring</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Healthcare AI governance does not end at deployment. AI systems require ongoing monitoring to detect performance degradation or drift.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Patient populations and disease patterns change over time. Models trained on historical data may become less accurate as conditions evolve.</p>

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Requirement 5: Bias Detection and Mitigation</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Healthcare AI governance addresses algorithmic bias that could harm vulnerable populations. Medical AI trained on biased data can perpetuate or amplify health disparities.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Regular audits for demographic bias are essential. Performance should be analyzed across age, gender, ethnicity, and socioeconomic factors.</p>

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Healthcare AI governance requires documented mitigation strategies when bias is detected. Simply acknowledging bias is insufficient – action is required.</p>
