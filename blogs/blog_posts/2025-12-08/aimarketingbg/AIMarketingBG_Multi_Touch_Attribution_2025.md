<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Model 1: Linear Attribution</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Multi-touch attribution in its simplest form distributes credit equally across all touchpoints. Every interaction receives the same percentage of conversion credit.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Linear models work well for awareness campaigns where each touchpoint builds incrementally. Multi-touch attribution with equal weighting provides baseline measurement.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Limitations include treating all touchpoints as equal when some clearly drive more influence. Consider linear attribution a starting point for more sophisticated models.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:spacer {"height":"40px"} -->
<div style="height:40px" aria-hidden="true" class="wp-block-spacer"></div>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Model 2: Time Decay Attribution</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Multi-touch attribution with time decay assigns more credit to recent touchpoints. Interactions closer to conversion receive higher weight than earlier ones.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>This model reflects the reality that recent touchpoints often have more influence on purchase decisions. Multi-touch attribution with time decay suits shorter sales cycles.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Model 3: Position-Based Attribution</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Multi-touch attribution with position-based weighting emphasizes first and last touchpoints. Typically 40% credit goes to first touch, 40% to last, and 20% distributed among middle interactions.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>This model recognizes that introduction and conversion touchpoints often matter most. Multi-touch attribution with position weighting balances awareness and conversion credit.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Customize weightings based on your business model. Some organizations find 30/30/40 or other distributions more accurately reflect their customer journeys.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
