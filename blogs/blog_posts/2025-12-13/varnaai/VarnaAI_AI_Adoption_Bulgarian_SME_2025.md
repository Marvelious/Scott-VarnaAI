<h2 class="wp-block-heading">EU AI Act Compliance for Bulgarian SMEs</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>AI adoption Bulgarian SME strategies must consider regulatory compliance. The <a href="https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai" target="_blank" rel="noopener">EU AI Act</a> establishes requirements for AI systems based on risk levels:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Minimal risk</strong>: Most SME AI applications (chatbots, analytics, content generation) face no specific requirements</li>
<li><strong>Limited risk</strong>: Transparency requirements apply - users must know they interact with AI</li>
<li><strong>High risk</strong>: AI for employment decisions, credit scoring, or critical infrastructure requires comprehensive compliance measures</li>
<li><strong>Prohibited</strong>: Certain AI applications (social scoring, manipulative AI) are banned entirely</li>
</ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Most Bulgarian SME AI use cases fall into minimal or limited risk categories with manageable compliance obligations. High-risk applications require more extensive documentation, testing, and oversight.</p>
