## NIS2 Compliance Bulgaria: The Reality for SMEs

Here's what **NIS2 compliance Bulgaria** actually requires for small and medium enterprises:

1. **Risk assessment** - Identify critical assets and threats
2. **Security measures** - Implement baseline controls (encryption, access management, backups)
3. **Incident response** - Have a documented plan for cyber incidents
4. **Supply chain security** - Verify your vendors' compliance
5. **Governance** - Assign responsibility to management level
6. **Reporting** - Notify authorities within 24 hours of significant incidents

If you're a Bulgarian company in sectors like energy, transport, healthcare, digital infrastructure, or critical manufacturing, you're required to comply. The European Commission estimates this affects 160,000 companies across the EU.

The Bulgarian cybersecurity authority will conduct audits. Non-compliance can result in fines up to €10 million or 2% of global annual turnover (whichever is higher). For reference, the <a href="https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/nis2-directive" target="_blank" rel="noopener">EU NIS2 Directive</a> explicitly states that management can be held personally liable.
