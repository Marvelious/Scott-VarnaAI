## 5. OUTBOUND STRATEGIES

### 5.1 Cold Email: LEGALLY PROHIBITED in Germany

**üö´ CRITICAL WARNING**: Cold email is **ILLEGAL** in Germany for B2B without explicit prior consent.

**Legal Framework**
- **DSGVO (GDPR)**: Requires consent for processing personal data
- **UWG (Act Against Unfair Competition)**: Prohibits unsolicited email advertising

**Key Difference from Phone**
- B2B phone: "Presumed consent" may apply (mutma√üliche Einwilligung)
- B2B email: Explicit consent REQUIRED - no presumed consent exception

**Penalties**
- DSGVO fines: Up to ‚Ç¨20 million or 4% of worldwide revenue
- UWG violations: Cease-and-desist (Abmahnung) + fines
- Typical SME fines: Four to five figures

**Valid Consent Forms**
- Verbal consent during phone call ("Please send me information")
- Double opt-in for newsletter registrations
- Contact form where contact was actively requested
- Business card exchange with explicit request for information
- Must document: date, time, and context

**Legal Alternatives**
1. **Compliant phone outreach** with documented presumed consent
2. **Content marketing and inbound**
3. **Trade shows and events**
4. **Paid advertising** (LinkedIn, Google)
5. **Social selling** (with platform compliance)

- "Trust is the demo in Germany"
- Lead with proof: controls met, audits passed, burden reduction
- Name German customers/partners upfront

**Longer Sales Cycles**
- B2B SaaS average: 2.5 months (general), longer in Germany
- Enterprise SaaS: 6-18 months (sometimes 12-24 months)
- Higher CAC and longer cycles expected
- Patience and relationship-building essential

**Local Presence Matters**
- Face-to-face meetings preferred
- German language shows respect and builds trust
- Partner with local agencies/representatives
- Consider German office or team members
