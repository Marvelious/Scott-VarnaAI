## 3. GDPR-Compliant Digital Marketing

### ‚öñÔ∏è Legal Requirements (2025)
**Sources:** Secureprivacy.ai, Usercentrics.com, Superoffice.com

**Key Compliance Areas:**

**1. Cookie Consent:**
- **Requirement:** Explicit consent before non-essential cookies fire
- **Implementation:** Consent Management Platform (CMP)
- **Best Practices:**
  - "Accept" and "Reject" buttons with equal visual weight
  - No pre-checked boxes
  - Granular controls (necessary, functional, analytics, marketing)
  - Server-side enforcement (block tags until consent)

**Recommended CMP:** Complianz (already installed on all 5 WordPress sites per CLAUDE.md)

**2. Email Marketing:**
- **Requirement:** Explicit opt-in (double opt-in recommended)
- **Implementation:** Confirmation email after signup
- **Mandatory Elements:**
  - Unsubscribe link in every email
  - Clear identification of sender
  - Privacy policy link
  - Data processing information

**Current Status:** All 5 brands have contact forms (COMPANY_INFO.md) but need double opt-in email sequence

**3. Data Minimization:**
- **Requirement:** Collect only necessary data
- **Application:** Lead forms should have 3-4 fields maximum (name, email, company, phone)
- **Avoid:** Birthdate, gender, personal preferences unless directly necessary

**4. Right to Erasure (RTBF):**
- **Requirement:** Process data deletion requests within 30 days
- **Implementation:** Documented process for removing data from:
  - Email marketing platform
  - CRM
  - Google Analytics
  - Marketing automation

**Financial Stakes:**
- **Maximum Fine:** ‚Ç¨20M or 4% of global turnover (whichever is higher)
- **Total GDPR Fines (2018-2025):** ‚Ç¨4 billion+
- **Average Fine:** ‚Ç¨500K-2M for SMEs

**Competitive Advantage:**
- **94% of customers** won't buy if data isn't protected
- **Average ROI on privacy spend:** 1.8x
- **Positioning:** "GDPR-Native" vs "GDPR-Compliant" (proactive vs reactive)
