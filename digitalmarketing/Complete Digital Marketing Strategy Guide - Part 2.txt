5. SEM (Search Engine Marketing) - Paid Search Mastery

Google Ads Foundation

Account Structure Best Practices





Organize campaigns by product/service line or geographic location



Create tightly themed ad groups (10-20 keywords maximum per group)



Separate brand campaigns from non-brand campaigns



Use naming conventions for easy management (e.g., "Brand_Search_US" or "Product_Display_EU")



Implement campaign-level settings (location, language, budget, bid strategy)



Set up conversion tracking before launching campaigns



Use shared budgets for related campaigns



Create separate campaigns for different networks (Search vs. Display)

Campaign Types Explained

Search Campaigns





Text ads appearing on Google search results



Intent-driven (users actively searching)



Best for: Lead generation, direct response, high-intent keywords



Average CPC: €0.50-€5.00 (varies by industry and location)



Conversion rates: 2-5% typical

Display Campaigns





Banner ads on 2+ million websites in Google Display Network



Best for: Brand awareness, remarketing, visual products



Lower CPC but lower conversion rates



Use responsive display ads for automatic optimization



Target by topics, interests, placements, or audiences

Shopping Campaigns





Product listings with image, price, merchant name



Essential for eCommerce businesses



Requires Google Merchant Center setup



Product feed optimization critical



Higher conversion rates than text ads for products

Video Campaigns





YouTube and video partner sites



Formats: Skippable in-stream, non-skippable, bumper ads, discovery ads



Best for: Brand awareness, product demos, storytelling



Cost per view (CPV) pricing model

Performance Max Campaigns





AI-driven campaigns across all Google properties



Single campaign accessing Search, Display, YouTube, Gmail, Discover



Requires conversion tracking and quality assets



Less control but potentially better performance



Best for: Businesses with clear conversion goals and quality creative assets

Discovery Campaigns





Visual ads on YouTube Home, Gmail, Google Discover



Swipeable carousel format



Best for: Visually appealing products/services



Lower funnel than Display, higher than Search

Keyword Strategy & Research

Keyword Research Process





Brainstorm seed keywords (core products/services)



Use Google Keyword Planner for search volume and competition



Analyze competitor keywords (SEMrush, SpyFu)



Identify long-tail variations (lower cost, higher intent)



Group keywords by theme/intent



Prioritize by search volume, competition, and business value



Create negative keyword list

Keyword Match Types

Broad Match





Triggers ads for related searches, synonyms, misspellings



Maximum reach but less control



Example: "ai software" triggers "artificial intelligence tools", "machine learning platform"



Use with Smart Bidding for best results



Monitor search terms report closely

Phrase Match





Ads show for searches that include the meaning of your keyword



More control than broad, more reach than exact



Example: "ai project management" triggers "best ai project management tool", "ai project management software pricing"



Good balance of reach and relevance

Exact Match





Ads show for searches with same meaning or intent



Most control, least reach



Example: [ai software] triggers "ai software", "software ai", "ai softwares"



Best for high-value, proven keywords



Higher conversion rates typically

Negative Keywords





Prevent ads from showing for irrelevant searches



Essential for budget efficiency



Examples for B2B: "free", "cheap", "jobs", "salary", "course", "tutorial"



Add weekly based on search terms report



Create negative keyword lists for reuse across campaigns

Keyword Organization Strategies

Single Keyword Ad Groups (SKAGs)





One keyword per ad group (all match types)



Maximum ad relevance and Quality Score



Better control over bids and ad copy



Time-intensive but effective for high-value keywords

Theme-Based Ad Groups





10-20 closely related keywords per group



Easier to manage at scale



Good for lower-value keywords



Maintain tight thematic relevance

Ad Copy Excellence

Search Ad Structure





3 headlines (30 characters each)



2 descriptions (90 characters each)



Display URL path (15 characters each)



Final URL (landing page)

Headline Best Practices





Include primary keyword in Headline 1



Highlight unique value proposition



Use numbers and statistics



Create urgency or scarcity



Ask questions that resonate



Test different angles (features vs. benefits)



Use title case for readability



Include brand name in Headline 3

Description Best Practices





Expand on headline promise



Include secondary keywords naturally



Clear call-to-action



Highlight differentiators



Address pain points



Use power words (proven, guaranteed, exclusive)



Test emotional vs. rational appeals



Include pricing if competitive advantage

Ad Extensions (Critical for Performance)

Sitelink Extensions





4-6 additional links below ad



Direct users to specific pages



Examples: "Pricing", "Case Studies", "Free Trial", "Contact Us"



Increase ad real estate and CTR



Use description lines for each sitelink

Callout Extensions





Short snippets highlighting features/benefits



Examples: "24/7 Support", "Free Shipping", "30-Day Trial", "No Credit Card Required"



25 characters each



Use 4-6 callouts minimum

Structured Snippet Extensions





Predefined categories (Services, Brands, Courses, etc.)



List specific offerings



Examples: Services: AI Automation, GDPR Compliance, Cloud Hosting

Call Extensions





Phone number displayed with ad



Click-to-call on mobile



Track calls as conversions



Set call hours to match business hours

Location Extensions





Show business address



Link to Google Business Profile



Essential for local businesses



Increases trust and credibility

Price Extensions





Display prices for products/services



8 items with prices



Pre-qualifies clicks



Higher intent traffic

Promotion Extensions





Highlight sales and special offers



Time-limited promotions



Increases urgency



Examples: "20% Off", "Free Shipping", "Buy One Get One"

Image Extensions





Visual elements in search ads



Square and landscape formats



Increases engagement and CTR



Use high-quality, relevant images

Landing Page Optimization for Ads

Message Match Principle





Headline on landing page matches ad headline



Consistent visual design and branding



Same offer/value proposition



Reduces bounce rate and increases Quality Score

Conversion-Focused Elements





Single, clear call-to-action above the fold



Minimal navigation (remove header menu for dedicated landing pages)



Trust signals (testimonials, logos, certifications, security badges)



Benefit-focused copy (not feature-focused)



Social proof (customer count, reviews, ratings)



Urgency elements (limited time, spots remaining)



Mobile-optimized design



Fast load time (under 2 seconds ideal)



Form optimization (fewer fields = higher conversion)



Exit-intent popup for abandoning visitors

Landing Page Testing





A/B test headlines, CTAs, images, form length



Test one element at a time



Run tests until statistical significance



Use Google Optimize or Unbounce



Document learnings and iterate

Bidding Strategies

Manual Bidding





Manual CPC: Set maximum cost-per-click for each keyword



Full control over bids



Time-intensive but precise



Best for: Small accounts, testing, tight budgets

Automated Bidding (Smart Bidding)

Target CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)





Set target cost per conversion



Google optimizes bids to hit target



Requires 30+ conversions in 30 days for learning



Best for: Lead generation with clear value per lead

Target ROAS (Return On Ad Spend)





Set target return on ad spend (e.g., 400% = €4 revenue per €1 spent)



Requires conversion value tracking



Best for: eCommerce with varying product values

Maximize Conversions





Get maximum conversions within budget



No target CPA set



Best for: New accounts building conversion data

Maximize Conversion Value





Get maximum conversion value within budget



Best for: eCommerce optimizing for revenue

Target Impression Share





Appear in specific position (top of page, absolute top)



Best for: Brand campaigns, competitive keywords



Can be expensive

Enhanced CPC (eCPC)





Manual bidding with automated adjustments



Google adjusts bids up/down based on conversion likelihood



Good middle ground between manual and automated

6. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) - The Future of Search

Understanding the GEO Landscape

What is GEO?
Generative Engine Optimization is the practice of optimizing content to be discovered, cited, and recommended by AI-powered search engines and chatbots including:





ChatGPT (OpenAI)



Google Gemini (formerly Bard)



Microsoft Copilot (Bing Chat)



Perplexity AI



Claude (Anthropic)



Meta AI



SearchGPT

Why GEO Matters Now





60%+ of searches becoming zero-click (AI provides direct answers)



Users trust AI recommendations



Traditional SEO alone insufficient for AI visibility



Early adopters gain authority positioning



AI training data includes web content (be the source)



Voice search increasingly AI-powered



Future of search is conversational and AI-mediated
