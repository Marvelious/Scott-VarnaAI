8. Social Media Marketing - Platform-by-Platform Strategy

Platform Selection Framework

Choose platforms based on:





Where your target audience spends time



Content format strengths (visual, text, video)



Business goals (brand awareness, lead gen, sales)



Resource availability (time, budget, team)



Industry norms and competitor presence

Platform Demographics Quick Reference

LinkedIn





B2B professionals, decision-makers



Ages 25-54, college-educated



Best for: B2B lead generation, thought leadership, recruitment



Content: Professional insights, industry news, company updates

Facebook





Broad demographic, ages 25-65+



Best for: Community building, local business, broad reach



Content: Community engagement, events, customer service

Instagram





Ages 18-44, visual-focused



Best for: Brand awareness, visual products, lifestyle brands



Content: High-quality images, Stories, Reels, behind-the-scenes

Twitter/X





News-focused, ages 25-49



Best for: Real-time engagement, customer service, thought leadership



Content: Quick updates, industry commentary, conversations

YouTube





All demographics, video consumption



Best for: Education, product demos, brand storytelling



Content: Tutorials, reviews, vlogs, webinars

TikTok





Ages 16-34, entertainment-focused



Best for: Brand awareness, viral content, younger audiences



Content: Short-form video, trends, entertainment

Posting Best Practices





Post 3-5 times per week minimum



Best times: Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM, 12 PM, 5-6 PM



Use native video (higher engagement than links)



Keep text concise (1,300 characters max, shorter better)



Include 3-5 relevant hashtags



Tag people and companies when relevant



Use document posts for lead generation



Pin important posts to top of page

LinkedIn Ads





Sponsored Content: Native ads in feed



Message Ads: Direct messages to prospects



Dynamic Ads: Personalized ads using profile data



Text Ads: Sidebar ads (lower cost)



Lead Gen Forms: Pre-filled forms for easy conversion



Targeting: Job title, company size, industry, seniority



Minimum budget: €10/day recommended



Average CPC: €2-€6 (higher than other platforms but quality leads)

Copy Best Practices





Compelling subject line (40-50 characters)



Preview text optimization (complement subject)



Personalization (name, company, behavior)



Conversational tone



Benefit-focused (not feature-focused)



Urgency and scarcity (when authentic)



Clear value proposition



Strong CTA copy (action-oriented)

Subject Line Formulas





Question: "Are you making these SEO mistakes?"

DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail)



DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication)



Custom sending domain



Dedicated IP (for high volume)



Proper DNS configuration

Best Practices





Warm up new IP addresses gradually



Maintain consistent sending schedule



Avoid spam trigger words



Balance text and images



Include plain text version



Easy unsubscribe option



Monitor bounce rates



Authenticate with ISPs

Avoid Spam Filters





Don't buy email lists



Use double opt-in



Avoid ALL CAPS and excessive punctuation!!!



Don't use misleading subject lines



Include physical address



Monitor spam complaints



Maintain good sender reputation



Test emails before sending

Email Metrics & KPIs

Key Metrics





Open Rate: 15-25% average (varies by industry)



Click-Through Rate (CTR): 2-5% average



Click-to-Open Rate (CTOR): 10-15% average



Conversion Rate: Varies by goal



Bounce Rate: Under 2% (hard bounces)



Unsubscribe Rate: Under 0.5%



Spam Complaint Rate: Under 0.1%



List Growth Rate: Track monthly



Email ROI: Revenue / email cost

Optimization Goals





Increase open rates: Better subject lines, send time optimization



Increase CTR: Compelling copy, clear CTAs, segmentation



Reduce unsubscribes: Better targeting, frequency management



Improve deliverability: List hygiene, authentication, engagement

Attribution Models

Attribution Types





Last Click: Credit to final touchpoint (default, often misleading)



First Click: Credit to initial touchpoint



Linear: Equal credit to all touchpoints



Time Decay: More credit to recent touchpoints



Position-Based: 40% first, 40% last, 20% middle



Data-Driven: Algorithm-based (requires sufficient data)

Multi-Touch Attribution
